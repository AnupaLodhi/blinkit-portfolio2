01 — Introduction

Converting Installs
into Orders

Quick commerce has transformed urban grocery shopping — deliveries in minutes, not hours. Blinkit leads this space with strong brand awareness and high app install rates among Gen Z users.

However, awareness alone doesn't drive revenue. Despite millions of installs, a significant portion of users never placed a single order. The gap between intent and action was costing Blinkit real revenue and inflating its Cost Per Acquisition.

This project designed and analyzed a complete growth marketing strategy to bridge that gap — converting passive app users into active, paying customers within the 18–28 age segment across metro cities.

  • Increase first-time order conversion rate among new users
  • Reduce Cost Per Acquisition (CPA) through smarter targeting
  • Improve early retention: repeat purchase within 14 days
  • Build emotional relevance with Gen Z through situational marketing

03 — Problem Analysis

Why Users Weren't
Ordering

📉

Low Conversion Rate

App installs were high but first-order conversions lagged significantly. Users explored the app but didn't proceed to checkout.

💸

Price Sensitivity

Student segment showed high price sensitivity. Without a compelling discount, many abandoned at the pricing stage.

⏳

No Urgency

Users only considered ordering when facing an immediate need — but no triggers were creating that urgency proactively.

🛒

Checkout Drop-offs

Friction in the checkout experience — too many steps, missing incentives at the final stage — caused last-minute abandonment.

Research Insights

Gen Z responds to urgency. Time-bound, situation-based messaging outperforms feature promotion in driving immediate action.

Impulse purchases spike with relatability. When content mirrors real daily situations, purchase intent increases sharply.

First-order discounts are decisive. Price sensitivity among students means a visible offer is often the final push needed.

Push notifications within 24 hrs work. Timely reminders after app exploration significantly improve conversion rates.

06 — Execution

How It Was
Implemented

Channel 01

Short-Form Content Marketing

  • 40+ Instagram Reels created around relatable scenarios
  • Strong 2-second hooks to stop mid-scroll
  • Real-life situations, not product features
  • Clear CTA: "Order now on Blinkit"
  • A/B tested hooks across audience segments
Channel 02

Campus Ambassador Program

  • 20 micro-influencers from target colleges
  • Unique referral codes per ambassador
  • ₹100 first-order discount for new users
  • Limited-time campus challenges for virality
  • Performance-tracked via Mixpanel & Firebase
Channel 03

Funnel Optimization

  • First-order discount highlighted on homepage
  • Reduced checkout steps to minimize friction
  • Exit-intent offer pop-ups at drop-off points
  • Push notification reminders within 24 hours
  • Personalized offer banners based on user behavior

09 — Conclusion

Key Takeaways

This project demonstrates that emotion-driven, urgency-based marketing combined with funnel optimization can significantly improve first-time order conversions in the quick commerce industry.

By repositioning Blinkit not as a delivery service, but as a solution to real daily emergencies, the campaign successfully converted passive awareness into measurable action. Situation-based content resonated with Gen Z far more effectively than feature-led messaging.

The results — a 32% lift in first orders, 18% CPA reduction, and 24% repeat purchase rate — confirm that understanding audience psychology and removing friction at every funnel stage is the most reliable path to growth.
